Quiche

Description

Quiche Lorraine is a classic French savory tart that epitomizes elegance and simplicity. Originating from the Lorraine region, this delectable dish features a buttery and flaky pie crust filled with a luscious mixture of eggs, cream, and a signature filling of crisp bacon or lardons. The creamy custard base is enriched with the smoky flavor of bacon, creating a harmonious blend of textures and tastes. Often enhanced with a touch of nutmeg or herbs, Quiche Lorraine is a timeless culinary delight that effortlessly marries the richness of its ingredients, resulting in a savory pie that is both sophisticated and satisfying.

Ingredients

  • Pie crust
  • Lardons
  • Eggs
  • Heavy cream
  • Milk
  • Salt and black pepper
  • Nutmeg
  • Swiss or Gruyère cheese

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and prepare a pie crust in a quiche pan.
  2. Cook 6-8 slices of bacon until crisp, then crumble them.
  3. In a bowl, whisk together 3 eggs, 1 cup of heavy cream, and 1/2 cup of milk.
  4. Season the egg mixture with salt, black pepper, and a pinch of nutmeg (optional).
  5. Sprinkle half of the crumbled bacon onto the pie crust.
  6. Pour the egg mixture over the bacon in the pie crust.
  7. Sprinkle 1 cup of shredded Swiss or Gruyère cheese on top.
  8. Add the remaining bacon on top of the cheese.
  9. Bake in the preheated oven for about 35-40 minutes or until the quiche is set and golden.
  10. Allow the Quiche Lorraine to cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving. Enjoy this delightful savory tart warm or at room temperature
